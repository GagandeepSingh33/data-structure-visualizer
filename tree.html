What is a Tree?

A tree is a non-linear, hierarchical data structure made of nodes connected by edges. The topmost node is called the root, internal nodes have one or more children, and nodes with no children are called leaves. Trees represent parent–child relationships and are used in searching, indexing, file systems, and many other applications.
