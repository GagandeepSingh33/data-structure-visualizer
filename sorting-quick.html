What is Quick Sort?

Quick Sort is a divide-and-conquer algorithm that picks a pivot element, partitions the array into elements smaller and greater than the pivot, and then recursively sorts those partitions.

After each partition step, the pivot lands in its final sorted position, and repeating this process on subarrays quickly orders the entire array.

Complexity & Legend

Time complexity: best and average cases O(n log n) with good pivots, worst case O(n²) for highly unbalanced partitions; space complexity about O(log n) due to recursion.
